Evaluating a Data Scientist job offer in Berlin means knowing where your number sits relative to the market. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks across four experience tiers — so you can see at a glance whether your offer is below, at, or above the going rate. All figures are in EUR, sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1.

## How to Read These Benchmarks

Salaries are expressed as three percentile points: p25 (bottom quarter of the market), p50 (the median), and p75 (top quarter). If your offer is below p25 for your experience tier, it is below market. At p50, it is squarely in line with the median. At or above p75, it is a competitive offer. All figures are annual gross base salary in EUR and do not include bonuses, equity, or benefits.

## Junior Data Scientist (0–2 Years Experience)

At the junior level, the Berlin market benchmark sits at 48,450 EUR at the median (p50). The lower quartile (p25) is 39,780 EUR, while the upper quartile (p75) reaches 58,650 EUR. An offer below 39,780 EUR warrants serious scrutiny. An offer above 58,650 EUR is strong for this experience band and suggests the employer is competing aggressively for early-career talent.

## Mid-Level Data Scientist (3–5 Years Experience)

Mid-level professionals in Berlin should benchmark against a p50 of 72,420 EUR. The p25 floor is 59,670 EUR and the p75 ceiling is 87,210 EUR. This is the tier where the widest range of offers tends to appear, so knowing your percentile position is especially valuable. If your offer is near or below 59,670 EUR and you have 3–5 years of solid experience, there is a clear case for negotiation.

## Senior Data Scientist (6–10 Years Experience)

Senior Data Scientists in Berlin command a median (p50) of 99,960 EUR. The p25 mark is 82,110 EUR and the p75 mark is 119,340 EUR. Crossing the six-figure threshold at this level is consistent with median market expectations, so an offer that falls meaningfully short of 99,960 EUR deserves a closer look. Offers at or above 119,340 EUR place you in the top quarter of the Berlin senior market.

## Staff or Lead Data Scientist (10+ Years Experience)

At the staff and lead level, the p50 benchmark is 129,540 EUR. The p25 is 105,060 EUR and the p75 is 157,080 EUR. At this seniority, total compensation packages — including bonuses and equity — can move the effective number significantly, but base salary alone should still clear 105,060 EUR to be considered market-rate. Data not available for equity or long-term incentive benchmarks at this tier.

## Key Factors That Shift Your Number

These benchmarks represent gross base salary and do not account for several variables that can meaningfully affect your total package. Industry sector (e.g., fintech vs. e-commerce vs. public sector), company size, tech stack specialization, and remote or hybrid arrangements can all push an offer above or below the percentile ranges shown. Data not available for sector-specific or company-size breakdowns within Berlin. Use the benchmarks here as your baseline, then factor in the full package when making your final evaluation.
