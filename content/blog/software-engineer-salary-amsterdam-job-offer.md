Evaluating a software engineer job offer in Amsterdam means knowing where your number actually sits in the market. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks by experience level so you can compare your offer against real data — not guesswork — before making a decision.

## Amsterdam Software Engineer Salary Benchmarks by Experience

All figures below are annual gross base salary in euros (EUR), sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1. The three percentile markers tell you how your offer compares: P25 is the lower end of the market, P50 is the midpoint, and P75 represents strong, above-median compensation.

Junior (0–2 years): P25 €35,000 | P50 €44,500 | P75 €55,500
Mid-level (3–5 years): P25 €62,000 | P50 €78,500 | P75 €100,000
Senior (6–10 years): P25 €97,000 | P50 €122,000 | P75 €147,000
Staff / Lead (10+ years): P25 €130,000 | P50 €160,000 | P75 €193,000

If your offer falls below the P25 for your band, it warrants scrutiny. At or above P75, you are looking at a strong offer relative to the Amsterdam market.

## How to Read Your Offer Against These Numbers

Start by identifying your experience band honestly — years of experience is the primary driver of where you fall. Then locate your offered base salary on the scale above.

Below P25: Your offer is in the bottom quarter of the market for your level. This does not automatically mean you should reject it, but you should understand why — early-stage startup equity, visa sponsorship, or a clear promotion path can sometimes justify a lower base.

P25 to P50: A fair but not exceptional offer. There may be room to negotiate toward the midpoint.

P50 to P75: A competitive offer. The company is paying above the median, which signals they are serious about attracting talent.

Above P75: A strong offer. Evaluate the full package carefully — high base salaries sometimes come with trade-offs elsewhere.

## What These Figures Include and Exclude

These benchmarks represent annual gross base salary only. They do not include bonuses, equity or stock options, pension contributions, holiday allowances, or other benefits that are common in Amsterdam tech compensation packages.

When comparing offers, always convert everything to a comparable basis. A lower base with significant equity or a strong pension match may outperform a higher base in total value, depending on your personal priorities and the company's stage.

## Amsterdam-Specific Compensation Context

Amsterdam is one of Europe's most active tech hiring markets, with a concentration of international companies, scale-ups, and enterprise technology teams. This creates meaningful variation in pay depending on the type of employer.

Data not available for: salary variation by company type (startup vs. enterprise vs. scale-up) in Amsterdam.

One factor unique to the Netherlands is the 30% ruling, a tax advantage available to qualifying international hires that effectively reduces taxable income for up to five years. If you are relocating from abroad and eligible, this can significantly affect your net take-home pay without changing your gross base salary — making an Amsterdam offer more valuable than the gross figure alone suggests. Confirm your eligibility with a tax adviser before factoring this into your decision.

## Negotiating Your Software Engineer Offer in Amsterdam

Knowing your market position is the foundation of any salary negotiation. If your offer is below P50 for your experience band, you have clear, data-backed grounds to ask for more. A straightforward approach: reference the market midpoint for your level and ask whether the company can move closer to it.

For senior and staff-level roles, the spread between P25 and P75 is wide — up to €50,000 or more. At these levels, negotiation has the highest potential impact and is almost always expected. Come prepared with your specific contributions, scope of responsibility, and the market data above.

For junior roles, base salary negotiation is still valid, but benefits, learning opportunities, and growth trajectory often carry more long-term weight than a few thousand euros at the starting line.
