Evaluating a software engineer job offer in Dublin means knowing where your number sits relative to the market. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks by experience level so you can assess any offer with confidence. All figures are in euros and reflect Dublin market data from CV_DATA 2026-Q1.

## Dublin Software Engineer Salary Benchmarks by Experience Level

The table below shows annual gross base salary percentiles for software engineers in Dublin across four experience bands. P25 represents the lower end of the market, P50 is the median, and P75 marks the upper quartile. An offer below P25 for your band warrants close scrutiny.

Junior (0-2 years): P25 €33,500 | P50 €44,000 | P75 €55,500
Mid-level (3-5 years): P25 €61,000 | P50 €77,500 | P75 €100,000
Senior (6-10 years): P25 €96,500 | P50 €122,000 | P75 €148,000
Staff / Lead (10+ years): P25 €130,000 | P50 €160,000 | P75 €192,000

## How to Read Your Offer Against These Numbers

Start by identifying your experience band, then locate your offer within the P25-P75 range. An offer at or above P50 is broadly competitive. An offer between P25 and P50 is below the median but not unusual, particularly at smaller companies or for roles with strong equity or bonus components. An offer above P75 is above the upper quartile and typically reflects either a high-demand specialisation, a large multinational employer, or both. If your offer falls below P25, use the benchmark data as a direct negotiation reference point.

## Junior Software Engineers (0-2 Years Experience)

The median gross base salary for a junior software engineer in Dublin is €44,000 per year. The middle 50% of the market sits between €33,500 and €55,500. If you are early in your career and have received an offer below €33,500, the data suggests meaningful room to negotiate upward. Offers approaching or exceeding €55,500 at this level are strong and typically come from well-funded tech companies or multinationals with structured graduate programmes.

## Mid-Level and Senior Software Engineers (3-10 Years Experience)

Mid-level engineers (3-5 years) see a significant salary step-up, with a median of €77,500 and an upper quartile of €100,000. This is a key negotiation window: the spread between P25 (€61,000) and P75 (€100,000) is nearly €39,000, meaning the company's budget flexibility is often substantial at this level.

Senior engineers (6-10 years) command a median of €122,000, with the upper quartile reaching €148,000. At this band, total compensation packages including bonus and equity become increasingly significant. Data not available for bonus or equity benchmarks in this dataset.

## Staff and Lead Engineers (10+ Years Experience)

At the staff and lead level, the Dublin market median sits at €160,000 gross base per year. The P25 floor is €130,000 and the P75 ceiling is €192,000. Engineers at this level are typically evaluating offers that include meaningful equity, performance bonuses, and leadership scope. Base salary alone is rarely the full picture, but the €130,000-€192,000 range provides a reliable anchor for base compensation negotiations. Data not available for stock or long-term incentive benchmarks.

## Key Factors That Affect Where Your Offer Falls in the Range

Several variables influence whether an offer lands closer to P25 or P75 for your experience band. Company size and funding stage are primary drivers: large multinationals and late-stage tech companies typically pay at or above the median, while early-stage startups may offer below-median base salaries offset by equity. Specialisation also matters. Engineers with expertise in areas such as machine learning, security, or distributed systems often attract offers in the upper quartile. Location within Dublin and remote or hybrid arrangements can also shift effective compensation. Data not available for sector-by-sector or specialisation-specific breakdowns.
