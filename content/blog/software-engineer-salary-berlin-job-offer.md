Berlin is one of Europe's most active tech hiring markets, but compensation varies widely by experience level and company. Before accepting any offer, you need to know where it sits relative to the market. The benchmarks below reflect annual gross base salary in euros for Software Engineers in Berlin, sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1.

## Berlin Software Engineer Salary Benchmarks by Experience Level

All figures below are annual gross base salary in EUR. P25 represents the lower end of the market, P50 is the median, and P75 reflects strong, above-median compensation. Use these as your reference points when evaluating an offer.

Junior (0-2 years experience): P25 = €39,000 | P50 = €47,500 | P75 = €57,500

Mid-Level (3-5 years experience): P25 = €58,500 | P50 = €71,000 | P75 = €85,500

Senior (6-10 years experience): P25 = €80,500 | P50 = €98,000 | P75 = €117,000

Staff / Lead (10+ years experience): P25 = €103,000 | P50 = €127,000 | P75 = €154,000

## What These Numbers Mean for Your Offer

An offer at or below P25 for your experience band means roughly three-quarters of Software Engineers in Berlin earn more. An offer near P50 is market-rate. An offer at or above P75 is genuinely strong and reflects either high demand for your specific skills, a well-funded employer, or successful negotiation. If your offer falls below P25, that is a clear signal to negotiate or to request additional context from the employer on their compensation structure.

## Junior Software Engineers in Berlin (0-2 Years)

The median gross base salary for junior Software Engineers in Berlin is €47,500 per year. Offers below €39,000 fall under the 25th percentile and should be scrutinised carefully. A strong junior offer reaches €57,500 or above. Early-career professionals should also factor in learning opportunities, mentorship, and equity or bonus structures, as base salary is only one component of total compensation. Data not available for bonus or equity benchmarks at this level.

## Mid-Level and Senior Software Engineers in Berlin

Mid-level engineers (3-5 years) see a significant step up in compensation, with a median of €71,000 and a P75 of €85,500. If you have 3-5 years of experience and are being offered less than €58,500, you are likely being offered a below-market package. Senior engineers (6-10 years) command a median of €98,000, with top-quartile earners reaching €117,000. At the senior level, the gap between P25 (€80,500) and P75 (€117,000) is substantial, reflecting how much specialisation, domain expertise, and negotiation skill can influence outcomes.

## Staff and Lead Engineers: Top of the Market

Software Engineers with 10 or more years of experience, or those in staff and lead roles, represent the upper tier of the Berlin market. The median salary at this level is €127,000, with P75 at €154,000. Even the P25 figure of €103,000 reflects a high floor for this cohort. If you are being recruited into a staff or lead role and the offer is below €103,000, it warrants a direct conversation about how the company benchmarks senior talent. Data not available for total compensation including long-term incentives or equity at this level.

## Key Factors That Shift Your Number in Berlin

These benchmarks reflect gross base salary and do not account for all variables that influence individual offers. Factors that can push compensation higher include specialisation in high-demand areas such as machine learning, distributed systems, or security; experience at well-known product companies; and strong competing offers. Factors that may result in lower base offers include early-stage startup environments (which may offset with equity), fully remote roles based outside Berlin, or roles with a narrower technical scope. Data not available for industry-specific or company-stage breakdowns within Berlin.
