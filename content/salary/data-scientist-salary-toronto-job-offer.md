Evaluating a data scientist salary Toronto job offer means knowing where your number sits relative to the market — not just whether it feels right. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks by experience level, drawn from CV_DATA 2026-Q1, so you can assess any offer with precision.

## How Toronto Data Scientist Salaries Break Down by Experience

Salary ranges for data scientists in Toronto vary significantly across career stages. All figures below are annual gross base salary in Canadian dollars.

Junior (0–2 years): P25 $57,630 | P50 $74,460 | P75 $95,370

Mid-level (3–5 years): P25 $103,020 | P50 $134,640 | P75 $172,380

Senior (6–10 years): P25 $167,280 | P50 $210,120 | P75 $255,000

Staff / Lead (10+ years): P25 $223,380 | P50 $275,400 | P75 $333,540

The P50 is the market midpoint — half of professionals at that level earn above it, half below. An offer at or above P50 is broadly competitive. An offer below P25 warrants scrutiny.

## What the Percentiles Actually Tell You

P25 marks the lower quartile. Offers here are not necessarily bad — they may reflect a smaller company, a narrower scope, or a role with strong upside in equity or bonus. P75 marks the upper quartile and typically reflects high-demand specializations, larger organizations, or roles with significant business impact. When reviewing your offer, identify which band your experience places you in, then locate your number on that range. If you are mid-level with three to five years of experience and your offer is below $103,020, that is a below-market position by this dataset.

## Key Factors That Move Your Number Within a Band

Base salary is only one dimension. Within any experience band, several factors push compensation toward P75 or pull it toward P25. Specialization in machine learning infrastructure, NLP, or computer vision commands a premium over generalist analytics roles. Industry matters too — financial services and technology firms in Toronto tend to pay toward the upper end of each band. Company size, the scope of your mandate, and whether the role involves people management all shift where your offer lands. Use the benchmarks as a floor check, not a ceiling.

## Total Compensation vs. Base Salary

These benchmarks cover annual gross base salary only. Total compensation for data scientists in Toronto often includes performance bonuses, RSUs or stock options at larger firms, and benefits such as extended health coverage and RRSP matching. A base offer at P50 paired with meaningful equity or a strong bonus structure may outperform a higher base with no variable component. When comparing offers, convert everything to a total annualized value before drawing conclusions.

## How to Use This Data When Negotiating

Bring the benchmark range into the conversation, not just a single number. If your offer is at P25 for your experience band, you have clear data to support a counter. Reference the P50 as a market midpoint and frame your ask around the value you bring relative to that anchor. Avoid negotiating against your current salary — negotiate against the market. If the role is senior and your offer is $167,280, you know the midpoint is $210,120 and the upper quartile is $255,000. That is a concrete, defensible position.
