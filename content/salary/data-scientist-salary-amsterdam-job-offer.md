If you are weighing a data scientist salary amsterdam job offer, knowing where your number sits in the market is the first step to negotiating confidently. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks for Amsterdam across four experience levels, sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1, so you can compare your offer against real market percentiles before you sign.

## How to Read the Salary Benchmarks

All figures below are annual gross base salary in euros (EUR), covering Data Scientist roles in Amsterdam. They are drawn from CV_DATA 2026-Q1 and reflect a role-specific multiplier applied to the Software Engineer baseline. Three percentile points are shown for each experience band: P25 (bottom quarter of the market), P50 (median), and P75 (top quarter). If your offer is below P25, it warrants scrutiny. At P50 you are at the market midpoint. At or above P75, the base is strong by Amsterdam standards. Benefits, bonuses, equity, and pension contributions are not included in these figures — factor those in separately when evaluating total compensation.

## Junior Data Scientist (0–2 Years Experience)

Junior Data Scientists in Amsterdam earn between €35,700 at the P25 and €56,610 at the P75, with a market median of €45,390 per year. If you are early in your career and your offer is below €35,700, the base is below the bottom quarter of the market. An offer near €45,390 is solidly in line with peers. Anything approaching or exceeding €56,610 is a strong starting package for this experience band.

## Mid-Level Data Scientist (3–5 Years Experience)

With three to five years of experience, the Amsterdam market widens considerably. The P25 sits at €63,240, the median at €80,070, and the P75 at €102,000. This is the band where negotiation leverage tends to be highest — a gap between P25 and P75 of nearly €39,000 means offer quality varies significantly by employer. If your offer is below €63,240, push back. At €80,070 you are at market. Crossing €100,000 at this level puts you in the top quarter. For context on how these figures compare across roles, see [Software Engineer Salary Amsterdam: Evaluate Your Job Offer](/blog/software-engineer-salary-amsterdam-job-offer).

## Senior Data Scientist (6–10 Years Experience)

Senior professionals with six to ten years of experience can expect a P25 of €98,940, a median of €124,440, and a P75 of €149,940. At this level, a below-median offer in Amsterdam is a meaningful signal — either the role is scoped below your seniority, or the employer's compensation philosophy lags the market. Use the P50 of €124,440 as your anchor in negotiations. ## Staff / Lead Data Scientist (10+ Years Experience)

At the staff and lead level, Amsterdam salaries reach their highest range in this dataset. The P25 is €132,600, the median is €163,200, and the P75 is €196,860. Offers below €132,600 for a genuine staff or lead scope should be questioned — either the title may not match the responsibilities, or the compensation is below market. The spread of over €64,000 between P25 and P75 reflects the wide variation in how companies value senior data leadership. Total compensation packages at this level often include equity or performance bonuses; data on those components is not available for this dimension.

## Key Factors Beyond Base Salary

Base salary is only one component of a job offer. When evaluating a Data Scientist role in Amsterdam, also assess: employer pension contributions (the Dutch system includes mandatory contributions that affect net take-home), any variable bonus structure, equity or stock options, the 30% ruling tax benefit if you qualify as an international hire, and remote or hybrid flexibility.
