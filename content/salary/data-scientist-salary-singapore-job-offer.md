## Singapore Data Scientist Salary Benchmarks by Experience Level

All figures below are annual gross base salary in Singapore Dollars (SGD), drawn from CV_DATA 2026-Q1. The three percentile markers — P25, P50, and P75 — show where the bottom quarter, the midpoint, and the top quarter of the market sit for each experience band. An offer below P25 warrants scrutiny; an offer above P75 is highly competitive.

Junior (0–2 years): P25 SGD 51,000 | P50 SGD 65,790 | P75 SGD 84,660

Mid-level (3–5 years): P25 SGD 91,290 | P50 SGD 116,280 | P75 SGD 148,920

Senior (6–10 years): P25 SGD 143,820 | P50 SGD 181,560 | P75 SGD 219,300

Staff / Lead (10+ years): P25 SGD 192,780 | P50 SGD 237,660 | P75 SGD 286,620

## What the Percentile Bands Mean for Your Offer

A percentile tells you how your offer compares to the broader market for your experience tier. If your offer lands at P50, half of data scientists at your level in Singapore earn less and half earn more — it is a fair market rate. An offer at P25 or below suggests room to negotiate upward. An offer at or above P75 indicates the employer is paying at a premium, which can reflect high demand, specialised skills, or a competitive hiring environment.

When assessing your offer, compare your years of direct data science experience — not total work experience — to the bands above. A candidate with three years of data science work but five years in adjacent roles typically benchmarks closer to the junior-to-mid boundary.

## Junior Data Scientists: Setting a Baseline

For professionals with zero to two years of experience, the Singapore market median sits at SGD 65,790. Offers below SGD 51,000 fall in the bottom quartile and may reflect roles with heavy training components, smaller firms, or limited scope. Offers approaching SGD 84,660 are in the top quartile for this band and are typically attached to well-funded tech companies or financial institutions with structured data science programmes.

## Mid-Level and Senior Data Scientists: Where Compensation Accelerates

The jump from junior to mid-level is substantial. The P50 for mid-level data scientists (3–5 years) is SGD 116,280 — roughly 77% higher than the junior median. This reflects the premium Singapore employers place on practitioners who can independently scope and deliver projects.

At the senior level (6–10 years), the P50 reaches SGD 181,560, with top-quartile earners at SGD 219,300. Senior roles typically carry expectations around mentorship, cross-functional stakeholder management, and ownership of modelling infrastructure, which drives the compensation premium.

## Staff and Lead Data Scientists: Top-of-Market Figures

At the staff or lead level (10+ years), the Singapore market median is SGD 237,660, with the top quartile reaching SGD 286,620. These roles typically involve setting technical direction, leading teams, and influencing product or business strategy. If you are evaluating an offer at this level, total compensation — including equity, bonuses, and benefits — becomes increasingly important relative to base salary alone.

## How to Use These Benchmarks When Negotiating

Start by identifying your experience band and locating your offer within the P25–P75 range. If your offer is below P50, you have a data-backed basis to request a higher base. Reference the market median for your band directly — employers respond better to specific benchmarks than to general appeals.

Beyond base salary, assess the full package: variable pay, equity vesting schedules, CPF contributions, and professional development budgets all affect total value. A base at P50 with strong equity upside may outperform a P75 base with no variable component, depending on your risk tolerance and career stage.
