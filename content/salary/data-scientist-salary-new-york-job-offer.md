If you're weighing a data scientist salary new york job offer, knowing where your number sits relative to the market is the first step. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks across four experience tiers, sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1, so you can assess your offer with real figures rather than guesswork.

## How to Read These Benchmarks

All figures represent annual gross base salary in USD for Data Scientist roles in New York City. They are organized by percentile: P25 is the lower end of the market, P50 is the median, and P75 represents strong, above-median compensation. Your offer should be evaluated against the tier that matches your years of experience. Total compensation — including equity, bonuses, and benefits — will sit above these base figures, but base salary is the most consistent point of comparison.

## Junior Data Scientist (0–2 Years Experience)

For early-career data scientists in New York, the market range is well-defined. The P25 base is $80,070 per year, the median (P50) is $103,020, and the P75 sits at $133,620. An offer below $80,070 warrants scrutiny unless offset by exceptional equity or learning opportunity. An offer at or above $103,020 is solidly competitive for this level.

## Mid-Level Data Scientist (3–5 Years Experience)

The mid-level tier shows a significant jump in compensation, reflecting the premium New York employers place on proven data science experience. The P25 is $143,820, the median is $185,640, and the P75 reaches $239,700. If you have three to five years of experience and are receiving an offer below $143,820, it is worth negotiating or benchmarking against competing offers.

## Senior Data Scientist (6–10 Years Experience)

Senior data scientists command substantial base salaries in New York. The P25 for this tier is $231,540, the median is $291,720, and the P75 is $353,940. At this level, the spread between P25 and P75 is over $120,000, meaning the specific company, industry, and scope of the role have a major impact on where an offer lands within the range.

## Staff or Lead Data Scientist (10+ Years Experience)

At the staff and lead level, New York data scientist salaries reach their highest tier. The P25 base is $311,100, the median is $383,520, and the P75 is $462,060. Professionals at this level typically carry organizational influence beyond individual contribution, and compensation reflects that. An offer below $311,100 at this experience level deserves a close look at the role's actual scope and seniority.

## How to Use This Data When Negotiating

Identify your experience tier, then locate your offer within the P25–P75 range. If your offer is at or below P25, you have a clear, data-backed case for negotiating upward. If it falls between P25 and P50, there is room to push toward the median. Offers at or above P75 are at the top of the market for base salary, though total compensation — equity vesting schedules, bonus structures, and benefits — should still be evaluated holistically.
