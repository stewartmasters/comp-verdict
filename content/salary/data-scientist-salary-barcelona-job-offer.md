## How to Read These Percentiles When Evaluating an Offer

Percentiles tell you where a specific salary sits within the distribution of real market pay. If your offer lands at P25, three-quarters of Data Scientists at your experience level in Barcelona earn more. If it lands at P75, you are in the top quarter of earners for that tier.

Use the P50 (median) as your baseline reference. Anything between P25 and P75 is within the normal market range. Offers below P25 or above P75 both deserve a closer look: the former may signal underpayment, the latter may come with higher performance expectations or trade-offs elsewhere in the package.

## Junior Data Scientists in Barcelona: What to Expect

For professionals with 0–2 years of experience, the median annual gross base salary in Barcelona is €28,560. The interquartile range runs from €23,460 to €35,190, meaning most entry-level offers will fall somewhere in that band.

If you are early in your career and receive an offer below €23,460, it is worth asking whether the role offers structured mentorship, meaningful project exposure, or a clear promotion timeline that could justify the lower starting point. An offer above €35,190 at this level is above the 75th percentile and is strong by market standards.

## Mid-Level and Senior Data Scientists: Where the Range Widens

The salary spread grows substantially as experience increases, reflecting the higher variance in responsibilities and specialisations at these levels.

At mid-level (3–5 years), the P50 is €51,000, with a range of €40,290 to €63,750. At senior level (6–10 years), the median rises to €74,460, spanning €61,710 to €90,270. The jump from mid to senior represents a meaningful step up, and professionals transitioning between these tiers should benchmark accordingly rather than accepting a linear increment on their current salary.

## Key Factors That Can Move Your Number

Salary benchmarks reflect base pay distributions, but several factors can legitimately push an individual offer above or below the median for their tier.

Industry vertical: Data Scientists in fintech, pharma, or enterprise SaaS typically command different rates than those in early-stage startups or public sector roles. Data not available for industry-level breakdowns within Barcelona.

Specialisation: Expertise in areas such as NLP, computer vision, or causal inference may attract a premium over generalist data science roles. Data not available for specialisation-level salary splits.

Company size and funding stage: Larger or well-funded companies often pay closer to or above the P75, while smaller organisations may compensate with equity or flexibility.

Remote or hybrid arrangements: Fully remote roles based in Barcelona but tied to higher-cost-of-living markets may offer salaries outside the local benchmark range entirely.
