## Milan Data Scientist Salary Benchmarks at a Glance

All figures below are annual gross base salary in euros. They reflect the 25th, 50th, and 75th percentiles for each experience band, giving you a clear picture of where the market sits rather than a single misleading average.

Junior (0–2 years): P25 €22,950 | P50 €30,600 | P75 €39,780
Mid-level (3–5 years): P25 €41,820 | P50 €55,590 | P75 €73,440
Senior (6–10 years): P25 €71,400 | P50 €89,250 | P75 €109,140
Staff / Lead (10+ years): P25 €95,370 | P50 €117,300 | P75 €141,780

If your offer falls below the P25 for your band, you have a strong case for negotiation. An offer at or above P75 is highly competitive for the Milan market.

## Junior Data Scientists (0–2 Years Experience)

Entry-level Data Scientists in Milan see a wide spread between the bottom and top of the market. The median offer lands at €30,600 gross per year, while the top quartile reaches €39,780. If you are being offered below €22,950, the offer is below what most employers in Milan are paying at this level and warrants a direct conversation about compensation.

## Mid-Level Data Scientists (3–5 Years Experience)

This is where salary progression accelerates meaningfully. The P50 for mid-level Data Scientists in Milan is €55,590, with top-quartile earners reaching €73,440. Professionals in this band typically have demonstrable project ownership and domain expertise, which justifies pushing toward the upper range when negotiating. If your offer is below €41,820, treat that as a red flag unless there are significant non-cash benefits offsetting the gap.

## Senior and Staff-Level Data Scientists (6+ Years Experience)

Senior Data Scientists (6–10 years) command a P50 of €89,250 in Milan, with the top quartile at €109,140. At the Staff or Lead level (10+ years), the median rises to €117,300 and the P75 reaches €141,780. These figures reflect roles with strategic influence, team leadership, or deep specialisation. If you are evaluating a senior or lead offer, compare it against both bands depending on the scope of the role described in the job description.

## How to Use These Benchmarks When Evaluating an Offer

Salary benchmarks are most useful when applied systematically. Start by confirming your experience band, then locate your offer against the P25–P75 range. An offer below P25 signals room to negotiate upward. An offer between P25 and P50 is fair but not exceptional. P50 to P75 is a strong offer. Above P75 is top-of-market.

Also factor in role scope: a Staff-level title with Senior-level responsibilities should be benchmarked at the Senior band, not Staff. Always ask for the total compensation breakdown, including bonuses, equity, and benefits, before comparing base salary alone.

## Key Takeaways for Negotiating Your Milan Data Scientist Offer

Milan's data science market shows a significant gap between junior and senior compensation, making career progression and role clarity critical negotiation levers. Use the P50 as your baseline expectation and the P75 as your target in a competitive negotiation. If an employer cannot meet P25 for your band, ask for a clear timeline to review compensation or consider whether the role aligns with your market value.
