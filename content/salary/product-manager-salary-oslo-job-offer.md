If you are weighing a product manager salary oslo job offer, knowing where your number sits relative to the market is the first step to negotiating effectively. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks for Product Managers in Oslo across four experience tiers, sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1. All figures are in Norwegian Krone (NOK).

## Junior Product Manager Salary Oslo (0–2 Years Experience)

For Product Managers early in their careers, the Oslo market shows a wide spread. The 25th percentile sits at NOK 356,400 per year, the median at NOK 459,000, and the 75th percentile at NOK 560,520. If your offer lands below NOK 356,400, it is below what most entry-level PMs in Oslo are earning. A figure near or above the median of NOK 459,000 is a competitive starting point for this tier.

## Mid-Level Product Manager Salary Oslo (3–5 Years Experience)

With three to five years of experience, compensation rises substantially. The p25 benchmark is NOK 637,200, the median is NOK 777,600, and the p75 is NOK 966,600. The gap between the 25th and 75th percentile at this level is nearly NOK 330,000, reflecting meaningful variation based on company size, sector, and scope of ownership. An offer below NOK 637,200 warrants close scrutiny unless offset by strong equity or other benefits.

## Senior Product Manager Salary Oslo (6–10 Years Experience)

Senior PMs in Oslo command significantly higher base salaries. The 25th percentile is NOK 937,440, the median is NOK 1,178,280, and the 75th percentile is NOK 1,429,920. At this level, total compensation packages often include performance bonuses and equity, so base salary alone may not tell the full story. Still, a base below NOK 937,440 for a senior role is a signal to negotiate or ask for transparency on the full package.

## Staff and Lead Product Manager Salary Oslo (10+ Years Experience)

At the staff and lead tier, Oslo benchmarks reach their highest range. The p25 is NOK 1,256,040, the median is NOK 1,545,480, and the p75 is NOK 1,865,160. Professionals at this level typically carry cross-functional leadership responsibility, and compensation should reflect that scope. If your offer is near the p25 for a role with significant strategic ownership, there is likely room to push higher. ## How to Use These Benchmarks When Evaluating an Offer

Identify your experience tier first, then locate your offer within the p25–p75 range. An offer at or above the median is generally competitive; below p25 suggests the employer is pricing below market. Factor in the full package — bonuses, equity, pension contributions, and remote flexibility all affect real compensation.
