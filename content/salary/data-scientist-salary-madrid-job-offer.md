If you are weighing a data scientist salary madrid job offer, knowing where your number sits relative to the market is the first step. This page breaks down annual gross base salary benchmarks for Data Scientists in Madrid across four experience bands, sourced from CV_DATA 2026-Q1. All figures are in euros (annual gross base).

## Madrid Data Scientist Salary Benchmarks by Experience Level

The table below shows the 25th, 50th, and 75th percentile annual gross base salaries for Data Scientists in Madrid. Use the median (p50) as your market anchor and the p75 as a signal of a strong offer.

Junior (0–2 years): p25 €24,480 | p50 €29,580 | p75 €37,230
Mid-level (3–5 years): p25 €42,330 | p50 €54,060 | p75 €66,810
Senior (6–10 years): p25 €64,770 | p50 €78,540 | p75 €94,350
Staff / Lead (10+ years): p25 €87,210 | p50 €106,080 | p75 €127,500

An offer at or above the p50 for your band is broadly competitive. An offer below the p25 warrants scrutiny unless offset by meaningful equity, bonus, or benefits.

## Junior Data Scientists (0–2 Years): What to Expect

Entry-level Data Scientists in Madrid see a wide spread between the bottom and top quartile. A p50 of €29,580 is the clearest market reference for candidates with up to two years of experience. If your offer is below €24,480 (p25), that places you in the bottom quarter of the market and you should push back or ask for clarity on the progression timeline. Offers approaching €37,230 (p75) are strong for this band and typically reflect in-demand specialisations such as NLP or computer vision, or a role at a well-funded tech company.

## Mid-Level Data Scientists (3–5 Years): The Widest Negotiation Window

The mid-level band shows the largest absolute gap between p25 (€42,330) and p75 (€66,810), a spread of over €24,000. This reflects genuine variation in company type, sector, and specialisation at this stage of a career. A p50 of €54,060 is a solid baseline for negotiation. If you are being offered below €42,330 with three or more years of hands-on experience, the offer is below market and you have a strong data-backed case to negotiate upward. ## Senior and Staff Data Scientists (6+ Years): High Variance, High Stakes

Senior Data Scientists (6–10 years) command a p50 of €78,540 in Madrid, with top-quartile earners reaching €94,350. At Staff or Lead level (10+ years), the p50 climbs to €106,080 and the p75 reaches €127,500. At these levels, total compensation packages often include performance bonuses, equity, and benefits that can materially shift the real value of an offer beyond base salary alone. Data not available for equity or bonus benchmarks in this dataset. Evaluate base salary against these benchmarks first, then assess the full package separately.

## How to Use These Benchmarks When Evaluating an Offer

Three practical steps when you receive an offer: First, identify your experience band and locate your p50. Second, calculate the gap between your offer and the p50 — this is your negotiation anchor. Third, factor in non-base elements (bonus, remote flexibility, equity, pension contributions) only after you have assessed the base. If your offer is below the p25 for your band, treat that as a red flag unless there is a documented and credible path to market rate within 12 months.
